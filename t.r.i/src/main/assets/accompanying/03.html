Opening Ceremony

Date: Sunday, June 7, 2015
Time: 18:00–20:00
Venue: Congress Hall, Prague Congress Centre
Price: Free for all registered participants

Held in the Congress Hall, the Opening Ceremony is open to all registred participants. Marc Remacle, CEORL-HNS President, shall begin the evening with a welcome speech. Immediately a erwards, the “CEORL-HNS Awards of Excellence” are to be presented.

Jan Betka, Congress President, shall deliver an unusual lecture titled, ”The Geniality and Deafness of Bedřich Smetana”, about one of the most famous Czech composers accompanied with samples of Smetana’s work. The Hradec Kralové Philharmonic Orchestra led by conductor Leoš Svárovský shall also perform the highlight of the evening, Antonín Dvořák’s New World Symphony. Join us for these unforgettable moments.

Presidential Dinner

Date: Tuesday, June 9, 2015
Time: 19:30
Venue: Prague Castle
Dress Code: Formal

The Presidential Dinner is taking place at the Prague Castle and is upon invitation only (tickets shall be checked at the entrance).

The Prague Castle, an ancient symbol of the Czech lands, is the most signifi cant Czech monument and one of the most important cultural institutions in the Czech Republic. According to the Guinness Book of World Records, the Prague Castle is the largest coherent castle complex in the world, with an area of almost 70,000 m2. A UNESCO World Heritage site, it consists of a large-scale composition of palaces and ecclesiastical buildings of various architectural styles, from Roman-style buildings from the 10 th century through to Gothic modifi cations in the 14 th century.

We have a unique possibility to listen to a performance by the Czech famous violist Jitka Hosprová.

Jitka Hosprová

The first Czech viola soloist and world renowned for her art, her albums present gems of Czech violin literature and the classical modern world. Jitka’s viola passion gives the audience a clear note of the existence of this overlooked instrument. Viola literature provides fascinating and inspiring composers to compose new pieces. It is stunning to look at this artist.

The whole evening is also accompanied by pianist Radim Linhart.

Radim Linhart

Pianist, composer, singer and bandleader. In 2004, he publically showed his skill by playing and singing 3210 songs and compositions by heart. With this achievement, he broke the world record in the Guiness Book.
